Split rationale:
TRAIN and TEST each contain six short Python Exercism practice exercises. Six keeps
early loop cost low while reducing the pass-rate quantum from all-or-nothing to
0.1667. The sets are disjoint and intentionally simple enough for repeated smoke
runs with a cheap model.

Metric contract:
pass_rate = (count(status == pass_1) + count(status == pass_2)) / total_exercises.
wall_ms is elapsed wall-clock time for the whole subset runner.

Subset mechanism:
The upstream runner has no arbitrary subset flag. The fixture loops
benchmarks/aider_polyglot.py once per exercise with --exercise <name> and aggregates
the per-exercise result JSON files.
